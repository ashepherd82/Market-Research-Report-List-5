<p><p>CFRP (Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer) materials are commonly classified into two main market types: thermosetting and thermoplastic. Thermosetting CFRP materials are formed by irreversible chemical reactions when heated, resulting in a rigid composite structure. On the other hand, thermoplastic CFRP materials become pliable when heated and solidify upon cooling, allowing for reshaping and recyclability. Both market types have unique properties and applications, with thermosetting materials offering high strength and rigidity, while thermoplastic materials provide flexibility and ease of processing.</p></p>
