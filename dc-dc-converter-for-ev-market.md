<p><p>DC-DC converters for electric vehicles come in two main types: isolated and non-isolated. Isolated converters provide electrical isolation between input and output, which enhances safety and allows for voltage step-up or step-down. They are commonly used in high-power applications. Non-isolated converters do not provide electrical isolation and are typically used for low-power applications where isolation is not required. Both types play a crucial role in converting and managing power efficiently in electric vehicles, contributing to their overall performance and reliability.</p></p>
