<p><p>Industrial autoclaves are commonly classified into two types: vertical and horizontal autoclaves. Vertical autoclaves are designed for sterilizing tall, narrow items, such as glassware, while horizontal autoclaves are ideal for bulkier items, like surgical instruments and laboratory equipment. Both types operate using high-pressure steam to kill bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens. The market for vertical autoclaves is driven by the need for efficient sterilization of delicate items, while the market for horizontal autoclaves is influenced by demand for sterilization of larger, bulkier items.</p></p>
