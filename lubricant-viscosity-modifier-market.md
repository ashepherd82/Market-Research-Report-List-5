<p><p>Lubricant Viscosity Modifiers are additives used in lubricants to regulate the viscosity of the oil, ensuring optimal functioning of machinery. There are three main types of viscosity modifiers on the market: OCP-Based, PMA-Based, and Styrenics-Based. OCP-Based modifiers are typically used in high-performance applications due to their excellent shear stability. PMA-Based modifiers offer improved low-temperature performance and viscosity index improvement. Styrenics-Based modifiers are known for their high viscosity index and excellent shear stability, making them suitable for a wide range of lubricant applications.</p></p>
