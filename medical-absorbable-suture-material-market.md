<p><p>Medical absorbable suture materials can be divided into two main types: synthetic and natural materials. Synthetic materials, such as polyglactin and polyglycolic acid, are manufactured in a laboratory and break down over time within the body. Natural materials, like catgut and silk, are derived from animal or plant sources and exhibit similar absorption properties. Both types of suture materials play a crucial role in wound closure and healing processes, providing healthcare professionals with a range of options to suit individual patient needs.</p></p>
