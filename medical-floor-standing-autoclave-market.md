<p><p>Medical floor-standing autoclaves come in two main types: automatic and semi-automatic. Automatic autoclaves are fully automated, requiring minimal human intervention, making them convenient and efficient for busy healthcare settings. They have pre-programmed cycles and may include features such as drying capabilities. On the other hand, semi-automatic autoclaves require some manual input from the operator, such as loading and unloading the sterilization chamber and selecting the appropriate cycle. They are typically less expensive than automatic autoclaves but may require more time and effort to operate.</p></p>
