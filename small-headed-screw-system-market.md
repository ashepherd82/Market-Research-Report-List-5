<p><p>The Small Headed Screw System market includes two main types: Headed and Headless screws. Headed screws have a visible head that allows for easy tightening and loosening using a screwdriver or wrench. These screws are commonly used in applications where the head needs to be visible or aesthetic. On the other hand, Headless screws have no visible head and are driven via an internal hex or torx drive. They are often used in applications where a sleek, flush finish is desired or where space is limited.</p></p>
