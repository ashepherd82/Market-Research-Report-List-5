<p><p>Superconducting power lines are classified into three types based on voltage levels: low voltage (LV), medium voltage (MV), and high voltage (HV). LV superconducting power lines are used for distribution networks within residential and commercial areas. MV lines are utilized for larger industrial and commercial facilities, while HV lines are deployed for long-distance transmission of electricity across grids. Each market segment caters to specific requirements and applications, with HV lines being the most advanced and efficient in terms of power transmission.</p></p>
